# One Piece — Haki

## What is Haki?

Haki is a power that exists in every living being in the One Piece world. It is a manifestation of the user's spirit — their willpower, fighting spirit, and life force. Unlike Devil Fruits, Haki is not consumed or granted by any external source. Anyone can develop Haki through training and willpower, though most people never awaken it.

Haki is invisible to the naked eye but can be sensed by other Haki users. It is the primary counter to Logia Devil Fruit intangibility — Haki-infused attacks can hit Logia users as if they were physical.

## Three Types of Haki

### Kenbunshoku Haki (Observation Haki)

Also called Mantra on Skypiea. Kenbunshoku Haki allows the user to sense the presence, emotions, and intentions of others. Advanced users can predict an opponent's movements a short time before they happen — essentially seeing the future. It also allows sensing people's locations over vast distances and even sensing emotions through walls or in darkness.

Notable Observation Haki users:
- **Usopp** — can use advanced Observation Haki to see far distances and sense auras
- **Katakuri** — can see several seconds into the future
- **Enel** — used his Mantra across all of Skypiea to detect everyone on the island

### Busoshoku Haki (Armament Haki)

Busoshoku Haki allows the user to coat their body or weapons in an invisible armor of hardened spirit. This armor is extremely hard and can damage Logia users who are otherwise intangible. The coating turns the area black.

Advanced Armament Haki allows the user to emit the Haki into a target without touching the surface — called "emission" or "internal destruction" Haki. This technique destroys things from the inside, making it effective against enemies with tough exteriors like Pacifista robots or enemies with very thick armor.

Notable Armament Haki users:
- **Rayleigh** — the Dark King, Silvers Rayleigh, taught Luffy Armament Haki during the timeskip
- **Garp** — can coat his fists to hit Devil Fruit users effectively
- **Luffy** — uses Armament Haki in Gear Fourth, coating his entire body

### Haoshoku Haki (Conqueror's Haki)

The rarest form of Haki, said to be the power of those with the qualities of a king. Only one in several million people are born with the potential to use Conqueror's Haki. It cannot be learned — only those born with it can awaken it. It allows the user to exert their willpower over others, knocking out those with weak wills. In large crowds, it can render most opponents unconscious instantly.

Advanced Conqueror's Haki allows the user to coat their attacks in it, like Armament Haki but far more powerful. This was shown by Kaido and Luffy in their battle on Onigashima.

Known Conqueror's Haki users include: Luffy, Zoro, Shanks, Whitebeard, Kaido, Big Mom, Roger, Rayleigh, Boa Hancock, Doflamingo, Chinjao, Sengoku, and Yamato.

## Haki and Devil Fruits

Haki does not nullify Devil Fruit powers — it adds physical substance to attacks so they can bypass intangibility. A Haki-coated punch still hits a Logia user but does not suppress their elemental transformation. Devil Fruit users can also coat their fruit-generated attacks or substances with Haki (e.g., Luffy coating his rubber fist with Armament Haki in Gear Fourth).

## Haki in Battle

The strongest fighters in One Piece combine all three types of Haki simultaneously. The Emperors (Yonko) and top Marines all master Haki to extreme levels. The key insight from Silvers Rayleigh to Luffy was: "Haki is the power to harm even those who have physical invulnerability from Devil Fruits."

Haki can be depleted with overuse — pushing it too hard causes the coating to fade and leaves the user exposed. Recovery takes time.
