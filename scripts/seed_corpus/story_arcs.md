# One Piece — Major Story Arcs

## East Blue Saga

The beginning of Luffy's journey to become King of the Pirates. He sets sail from his home island and recruits the first members of his crew.

**Key arcs:**
- **Romance Dawn**: Luffy sets sail and rescues Coby from the pirate Alvida
- **Syrup Village**: Usopp joins; the Going Merry is acquired
- **Baratie**: Sanji joins after Luffy fights Don Krieg
- **Arlong Park**: Nami's backstory revealed; Luffy defeats Arlong and frees Cocoyasi Village
- **Loguetown**: the crew visits the town where Roger was born and executed

## Alabasta Arc

One of the most beloved arcs in the series. The crew travels to the desert kingdom of Alabasta to stop the Warlord Crocodile from overthrowing the kingdom using his organization Baroque Works. Nico Robin (then Miss All-Sunday) was Crocodile's partner. After the arc, Robin joins the crew. This arc introduces the Poneglyph and the concept of the Ancient Weapons.

## Skypiea Arc

The crew reaches Skypiea, an island in the sky above the Grand Line. They face off against Enel, a man who ate the Rumble-Rumble Fruit and declared himself a god. Enel's Mantra (Observation Haki) made him nearly unbeatable — except against Luffy, whose rubber body is immune to lightning.

## Water 7 / Enies Lobby Arc

Considered by many fans the greatest arc in the series. The crew faces CP9 — the World Government's elite assassins — to rescue Nico Robin, who had surrendered to protect the crew. Luffy declares war on the World Government by burning the flag at Enies Lobby. Luffy first uses Gear Second and Gear Third in this arc. The Going Merry is given a Viking funeral.

## Marineford Arc (Summit War)

The war to save Portgas D. Ace from execution at Marine Headquarters. The Whitebeard Pirates and their allies declared war on the Marines. Key events:
- Whitebeard is killed after receiving 267 sword wounds, 152 gunshots, and 46 cannonball hits — and never turned his back on the enemy
- Ace is killed by Akainu's magma fist protecting Luffy
- Shanks arrives to end the war
- Luffy falls into a coma from the trauma

This arc permanently changed the world's balance of power and scarred Luffy deeply, leading him to train for two years before continuing his journey.

## Fishman Island Arc

The crew reunites after two years of training and reaches Fishman Island, an underwater kingdom. They face the New Fishman Pirates led by Hody Jones. Luffy defeats Hody and declares the island under his protection. Jinbe formally declines to join until he can leave his previous obligations.

## Dressrosa Arc

The crew takes down Donquixote Doflamingo, a former World Noble and Warlord who had enslaved the people of Dressrosa for a decade using his Bird Cage technique. Luffy first uses Gear Fourth: Boundman. Law and Luffy form a Pirate Alliance. Sabo returns alive and claims the Flame-Flame Fruit. The Straw Hat Grand Fleet is formed.

## Whole Cake Island Arc

Luffy leads a team to Whole Cake Island — Big Mom's territory — to retrieve Sanji, who was forced into a political marriage by his family. Key events:
- Sanji's Vinsmoke family background and genetic modifications revealed
- Big Mom's horrifying past with Mother Caramel revealed
- Luffy battles Katakuri, Big Mom's strongest son, who can see the future with Observation Haki — Luffy learns to do the same
- Germa 66 helps the crew escape

## Wano Arc

The biggest arc in the series. The crew travels to the isolated country of Wano to liberate it from Kaido — one of the Four Emperors — and his ally Shogun Orochi. Key events:
- Luffy is imprisoned in Udon prison and trains to master advanced Armament Haki
- The truth about Joy Boy, the Void Century, and Luffy's Devil Fruit is revealed
- Luffy achieves Gear 5 — his Zoan awakening as the Sun God Nika
- Kaido is defeated and sent deep into the earth
- Big Mom is defeated by Kid and Law
- Wano opens its borders for the first time in centuries
- Luffy is declared one of the Four Emperors
- Zoro defeats King, Kaido's right hand, and unlocks advanced Conqueror's Haki coating

## Egghead Arc (Current)

The crew arrives at Egghead Island — the island of the future — home to the genius scientist Dr. Vegapunk. Key revelations:
- Vegapunk reveals a world-shaking message to be broadcast globally
- The Void Century's secrets begin to surface
- The Five Elders (Gorosei) arrive personally, suggesting the stakes are existential for the World Government
- Kuma's tragic past is fully revealed
